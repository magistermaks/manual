Shooting: Ships can shoot in two ways, either using torpedoes (bombs) or ammunition. Torpedoes can only go in a straight line from the ship (up to 4 tiles) and always hit the first thing they encounter, be it another ship, a bridge, unit, or factory (yes they can go onto land). They work similarly to bombs but destroy only a single tile. Shooting with ammunition works similarly to doing the same with normal units, but has the range of 2 in all directions (including diagonally) and can't be blocked (it always hits the target, even if there is something in the way). A ship can use both ammunition and torpedoes in the same turn.

The red outline around ship 1 shows its torpedo firing range, and the red outline around ship 2 shows its ammunition fire range.

Shield: Ships of tiers 2 and 3 can purchase a so called "shield module" for 5m while in a harbor. It allows you to shot down bombs that fall in a range of 2 tiles (without diagonals) with your own bombs (the ones stored on the ship).

The red outline around ship 1 shows the range of its "shield module".

Shield module is denoted in the ship list by adding a letter 's' in the tier column after the tier number. So Ship of tier 2 with a shield would have a tier '2s'.

Sinking: When the ship sinks (or loses a tier causing some people to be lost as they don't fit anymore) and is within 2 tiles of any land, you can evacuate all the to-be-lost units onto the land, as long as they fit in that 2 tile area (same area as defined by the ship's shooting range).

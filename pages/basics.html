GETTING STARTED

the basics of the game

The two main resources you will need to manage are (the creatively named) Materials and Units. Every turn you can summon one new unit (for example \UNIT\) from your capital (\CAPITAL\) inside your country borders.

New units can only be summoned inside your country and at most 10 tiles (you can count diagonally) from the capital.

Learn how to move your units in the units chapter.

The second resource - materials - can be obtained by building mines on top of deposits (this can be done for free) and increasing the income field in your statistics. But to do that you will need to take over the land they are on, by colonizing it.

The unit you select to colonize with must have been standing there at the end of the previous turn (you cannot colonize with a moved or freshly summoned units) but you can still move it after colonizing or shot with it at any point.

After you made the selection and announced it to other players roll the K6 dice. If the number is odd your unit dies (unless it has armor - then it just losses it). Additionally if you roll \2\ you gain extra terrain according to the "large" pattern.

The border after rolling \2\. Remember that you can only colonize once per turn and that you cannot take over the territory of other countries (there is a separate colonization method for that). Learn more in the colonization chapter.

You can also sometimes find Surface Deposits (\SURFACE_DEPOSIT\) on the map, once they are inside your country you can at any point collect them and add 2m to your warehouse per every erased tile.

At the beginning of every turn add the income to your warehouses (see statistics), if not everything fits the excess will be lost, so be sure to have enough storage space for your materials by building a warehouse.
